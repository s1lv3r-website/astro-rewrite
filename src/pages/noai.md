I am strongly opposed to any[^1] form of AI being used, especially generative AI, be it text, image, video, or anything else. This page will serve as a living document and writeup of the reasons I am opposed to the use of generative AI.

[^1]: "Any" here generally means generative -- there are some _more valid_ uses for AI such as medical purposes (see ["FAQ / Counterpoints"](#faq--counterpoints)), but the jury is still out on those usecases as well.

> **Note:** This document is not finished. Any grayed-out sections are yet to be started/finished.

## Why?

For me there are more than a few reasons, but I'll separate them into four major categories of concern: The human factor, environmental, ethics and societal, and financial. While the human factor and ethics are closely related, they feel distinct enough for me to separate them into two separate categories.

For simplicity's sake, whenever I refer to plain "AI", think Generative AI. Machine Learning (ML) is distinctively different, and not comparable to GenAI.

### The Human Factor

To me, the human factor is the most important argument against AI. It has the very noticeable impact of hampering your creative abilities, as well as affecting your critical thinking skills. It also creates a very false narrative that creating something is easy and requires no human input other than a prompt.

Content generated by AI also has a _very_ distinct lack of personality, and follows very set typing patterns.

There is also the big issue of the "dead internet theory". Chatbots are being used more and more instead of people visiting sites organically. This means content that someone spends hours, days, weeks, or even longer on isn't actually being read at all. Further, a lot of pages block AI bots, making their content inaccessible to everyone using AI as their search engines.

### Ethics and societal

On the more ethical problems, and problems affecting all of society there is the issue of bots changing how we write, and schools having to use their time and energy on "beating AI" instead of doing teaching and improving teaching materials.

A good example of AI changing how we type is the em-dash. Before the era of chatbots, the em-dash was a sign of someone who knew how to type properly. Now, a lot of people are using it as an instant "aha!" when trying to detect AI content, even if said content was written completely organically by a human being.

## FAQ / Counterpoints

### "But x, y, or z!"

This page isn't meant to list and discuss every type of AI or reason against AI use. I have listed the biggest arguments against AI I could think of, however there are certainly fields which can benefit from the technologies and ML evolutions happening because of all this. Some of those are listed below, but again: This page is not exhaustive.

### How about the medical sector?

There are plenty of researchers trying to achieve good things through AI and Machine Learning, especially within the medical sector there are some fascinating use cases:

- [Machine learning in medicine: what clinicians should know - PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10071847/)
- [Machine Learning in Medicine - A Virtual Seminar Series](https://ml-in-medicine.org/)
- [Machine learning in medical applications: A review of state-of-the-art methods - ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0010482522002505)
- [AI in diagnostic imaging: Revolutionising accuracy and efficiency - ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666990024000132)
- [The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Cancer Diagnosis and Treatment: A Review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12449654/)

AI and other previously inaccessible automated analysis methods have been used for detecting and diagnosing forms of cancer and other tumors. These are clearly groundbreaking pieces of research, however despite these I am still critical to GenAI use in medicine. This applies to text and chat services especially, largely due to the potential for hallucinations and otherwise inncorrect or directly harmful information, as well as the massive privacy implications in regards to HIPAA, GDPR, and other privacy frameworks.

These concerns obiously apply to more than just medicine, but that is where I am the most concerned due to how bad companies are at data security already.
